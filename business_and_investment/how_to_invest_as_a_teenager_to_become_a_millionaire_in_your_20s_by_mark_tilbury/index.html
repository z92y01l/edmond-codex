How To Invest as a Teenager To Become A Millionaire in Your 20s by Mark Tilbury

Age 13: The Power of Time in the Market​

The most important factor in investing is time in the market.

  • Open a Custodial Account: Since you are not of legal age to invest, convince a parent or guardian to open a custodial account (like a Junior Stocks and Shares ISA in the UK or a UGMA/UTMA in the USA).
  • Invest Simply: A suggested strategy is to invest in a simple, low-cost S&P 500 Index Fund, which spreads your money across over 500 of the biggest public companies in the USA.

Age 14: Freedom to Explore​

At this age, you have the advantage of not paying monthly bills, giving you the freedom to try different ways to make money and discover your talents.

  • Try Everything: Go out and try a wide range of activities, from competitive sports to pressure washing driveways. Not everything has to be about making money; you can learn discipline and useful skills.
  • Double Down on Talent: When you find something you are naturally good at or enjoy getting better at, focus on it and make it your mission to become one of the best.

Age 15: Stashing Cash​

This stage is about building your starting capital.

  • Ask for Cash: Ask for cash for birthdays and holidays instead of physical presents.
  • Get a Saturday Job: A part-time job, even in retail, helps you earn extra money and learn useful social skills.
  • The "Stash" Mentality: Use the word "stash" instead of "save." View this money not for spending on short-term wants, but as a launchpad to make even more money, making the start easier than starting with $0.
  • Apply for a Provisional Driving License: Get this done, even if you don't intend to use it right away.

Age 16: Stacking Skills​

Focus on developing skills that will be valuable when you enter the real world.

  • Stack Your Skills: Pick a handful of skills you're good at (e.g., selling, design, mechanics) and start investing money into getting better at them, trusting they will link up later.
  • Invest in Equipment/Tools: Invest in tools and equipment over expensive courses to learn and practice valuable skills like video editing or coding.
  • Develop Value: The goal is to come into the real world with actual value to offer, which reduces the amount of training a company needs to give you.

Age 17: Get Your Driving License​

Passing your driving test is essential for creating future side-hustle opportunities.

  • Pass Your Driving Test: Not being able to drive is one of the biggest obstacles to starting a side hustle, as you may miss opportunities with clients who live far away.
  • Buy a Starter Car: Once you learn to drive, use some of your stashed money to buy a cheap starter car, removing major barriers to working.

Age 18 and Older: Checklist for Financial Success​

Once you turn 18, you are an adult and can take seven key steps to set yourself up for success:

  1. Open Your Own Bank Accounts: Open two accounts: a Current/Checking Account for money flow and a High-Interest Savings Account to build an emergency fund of 3 to 6 months of living expenses.
  2. Get a Credit Card: This is the perfect tool to build your credit score. Use it to pay for things you would normally buy in cash, and then pay the balance off in full every month to avoid interest.
  3. Open an Investing Account: Open a tax-advantaged investment account like a Roth IRA (USA) or Stocks and Shares ISA (UK) to avoid paying taxes on your investments.
  4. Carefully Consider University: Only go to university if your chosen career (e.g., doctor, nurse, teacher) requires a degree. For trades, entrepreneurship, or tech, practical experience is often more valued. Consider an apprenticeship as a paid alternative.
  5. Avoid Bad Debt: Avoid "consumer debt" on things that do not create wealth, such as financing a car. Use "good debt," like a mortgage or a business loan, strategically to acquire assets that can increase your net worth.
  6. Start a Side Hustle: Use the valuable skills you developed to start a service-based side hustle (e.g., copywriting, video editing, web development). Use your day job for motivation and as a launchpad for your greater goals.
  7. Invest for the Long Term: Leverage compound interest, which means earning interest on your initial investment and on the interest you've already earned. Starting at age 18 versus age 28 can result in a difference of hundreds of thousands of dollars due to the power of those extra ten years of compounding.
